Gaze

2021

  • Editorial Design

Gaze is a project that recalls family memories. I interviewed my parents and my grandparents about their experience as parents of international students. My two uncles studied abroad at the end of the 20th century, they were the typical international students when China just opened the door to the rest of the world. Two decades later, I went to study abroad when people in both China and the United States were already familiar with international students.

In recent years, the uncertainties of China and the United States relationship was the most concerning topic among international students and their families. Meanwhile, the Covid-19 pandemic changed people's mind about many things in life. Therefore I think it was a perfect opportunity to rethink what sending children to study abroad means to a Chinese middle class family. The book was made up of two parts: an interview with my grandfather and an interview with my parents. The pages of both sections were separated into two columns for bilingual content.

I concluded five questions as an outline of the interview. These are the questions that I wanted to ask my family, but never found the right time to do so. I asked my grandfather and my parents the same questions, and displayed their answers alongside photos for comparison. After the interview, I transcribed the conversation and rearranged them so they are easy to read. I interviewed my family in Chinese, and I translated the content into English.
